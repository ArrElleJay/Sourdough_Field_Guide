Starter basics

Sourdough is science.Annoying, delicious science.

Temperature changes everything

Warm starter and dough ferment faster. Cool starter and dough take longer. Use time as a guide, not a personality test.

Ratios set timing

A 1:1:1 feed peaks faster and tastes sharper. A 1:10:10 feed peaks slower and tastes much milder.

Add-ins are not neutral

Sugar, honey, and fruit can speed things up. Cinnamon, garlic, fat, and eggs can slow things down. Rye, cocoa, whole grains, and seeds absorb water.

Bulk ferment is the hard part

Times are estimates. A shot glass sample can help you see when the dough has risen enough without guessing like a kitchen goblin.

Sample schedule

Basic loaf schedule

Night before, 9:00 PMFeed starter if using 1:3:3 or greater. Leave covered at room temp.
8:00 AMMix dough with active starter.
8:00 to 8:30 AMRest 30 minutes.
8:30 to 9:15 AMDo 4 stretch and folds, 15 minutes apart.
9:15 AM to 1:00 PM-ishBulk ferment until puffier, smoother, and increased about 75 to 100%.
1:00 PM-ishShape loaf.
Same-day bakeProof 1 to 2 hours, preheat Dutch oven to 500°F, bake covered 25 minutes, reduce to 450°F and uncover about 15 minutes, until 200 to 210°F inside.
Fridge bakeRefrigerate after shaping. Bake straight from the fridge. Do not bring to room temp.
