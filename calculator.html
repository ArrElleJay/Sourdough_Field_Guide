Calculators

Bread math without the spreadsheet spiral.

Use these for starter feedings, recipe scaling, hydration, baker's percentages, and turning vague cup measurements into grams.

Tool 01

Starter Feeding Calculator

Tell it how much active starter you need and how much you want left in the jar. It gives you the seed starter, flour, and water to feed.

Feed this

Seed starter18g
Flour54g
Water54g

A 1:3:3 feed gives the starter more food and usually a longer window before peak.

Tool 03

Hydration Calculator

Hydration is the water compared to the flour. This includes the flour and water inside your starter, because starter is not imaginary.

Dough hydration

Total flour525g
Total water375g
Hydration71%

This is a moderately wet dough. Manageable, but it will still try to humble you if you rush it.

Tool 04

Baker's Percentage Calculator

Flour is always 100%. Everything else is measured against flour. This is mostly useful when you want to compare recipes or see what a dough is really doing.

Quick read

70% water, 20% starter, and 2% salt is normal sourdough territory.

Tool 05

Measurement Converter

For recipes that still say things like “1 1/2 cups flour,” because apparently we have not suffered enough.

Use this to translate a recipe, then weigh the ingredient. Do not use it as an excuse to avoid buying a scale.

About the converter: cup-to-gram values are approximate and ingredient-specific. The flour baseline follows King Arthur Baking's 120g per cup standard for all-purpose flour. When a recipe gives its own gram weights, use the recipe's weights.
