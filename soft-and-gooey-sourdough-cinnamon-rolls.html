Soft & Gooey Sourdough Cinnamon Rolls

Makes 8 rolls | Overnight enriched dough | Bake 350°F for 35 to 40 minutes

Soft & Gooey Sourdough Cinnamon Rolls

Makes 8 rolls | Overnight enriched dough | Bake 350°F for 35 to 40 minutes

Back to recipes

Field note

These are an overnight enriched sourdough dough, not a loaf in a spiral costume. Milk, butter, egg, and sugar make the dough softer, but they also change the timing. Give the dough the long overnight rise, then roll, fill, proof, and bake in the morning.

The dough should be soft and workable. If it fights the rolling pin, let it rest. If it feels hot, sticky, and ridiculous, pause before adding more flour.

Before you start

  1. Use active starter, not discard. The recipe depends on the starter doing real lifting overnight.
  2. Let the melted butter and milk cool slightly before mixing. Hot dairy and butter can make the dough unpleasantly sticky.
  3. The 84g softened butter filling is the cleaner option because it helps keep the cinnamon sugar from leaking out during baking.
  4. This is best planned as an overnight recipe: dough in the evening, shape and bake in the morning.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the milk and butter. Melt the butter and combine it with the milk. Let the mixture cool slightly before it goes into the dough.
  2. Start the dough. In a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, mix the egg, active starter, and sugar until combined.
  3. Add the milk mixture. With the mixer running on low, slowly pour in the cooled milk and butter.
  4. Add flour and salt. Mix until a rough, sticky dough forms, about 1 minute. Scrape down the bowl.
  5. Rest 30 minutes. Cover the bowl and let the dough rest. This gives the flour time to hydrate before kneading.
  6. Knead. Switch to the dough hook. Knead on medium-low for 6 to 8 minutes, until the dough is soft, supple, and pulling away from the bowl. Add a small amount of flour only if it is truly unmanageable.
  7. Bulk rise overnight. Move the dough to a buttered bowl, cover, and let rise overnight until doubled, about 8 to 12+ hours at 67 to 68°F.
  8. Optional fold. About 30 to 60 minutes into the bulk rise, you can do one stretch and fold around the bowl to build structure. This is helpful, not mandatory.
  9. Prepare the pan. In the morning, line a 9-inch pan with parchment.
  10. Rest before rolling. Turn the dough onto a lightly oiled and floured counter. Pat into a rough rectangle and let rest 10 minutes.
  11. Roll the dough. Roll into a rectangle about 16 by 13 inches. If the dough resists, let it rest 5 to 10 minutes and try again.
  12. Make the filling. Mix softened butter, sugar, cinnamon, and flour until spreadable.
  13. Fill. Spread the filling over the dough, leaving about a 1/2-inch border.
  14. Roll and cut. Starting from the long side, roll the dough into a tight log. Cut into 8 rolls, about 2 inches each.
  15. Second rise. Place the rolls in the lined pan. Cover and rest 1 to 2 hours, until puffy.
  16. Bake. Bake at 350°F for 35 to 40 minutes, checking around 30 minutes. The tops should be lightly golden.
  17. Cool. Let the rolls cool in the pan for 15 minutes so the butter can settle back into the dough.
  18. Glaze. Beat the softened butter, cream cheese, powdered sugar, and enough milk to loosen it. Spread over warm rolls.

How to tell they are ready

  1. After bulk rise: dough should be doubled and soft, not dense.
  2. Before baking: shaped rolls should look puffy and expanded.
  3. After baking: tops should be lightly golden, and the center should not look wet or collapsed.
  4. Before glazing: wait 15 minutes so the rolls are warm, not molten.

Quick recipe version

  1. Evening: mix dough with active starter, milk, butter, egg, sugar, flour, and salt.
  2. Rest 30 minutes, then knead 6 to 8 minutes.
  3. Bulk rise overnight, 8 to 12+ hours at 67 to 68°F, until doubled.
  4. Morning: turn out dough, rest 10 minutes, roll to about 16 by 13 inches.
  5. Spread filling, roll into a log, and cut into 8 rolls.
  6. Proof 1 to 2 hours, until puffy.
  7. Bake at 350°F for 35 to 40 minutes. Cool 15 minutes. Glaze warm.
Notes: This dough has butter, milk, egg, and sugar, so it will not behave like lean bread dough. Let it rest when it pushes back. Adding flour every time it annoys you is how you get dry rolls.
