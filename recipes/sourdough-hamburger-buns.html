Sourdough Hamburger Buns

Makes 6 buns | Bake 375°F for 25 minutes | Rise about 6 hours plus 1 to 2 hours

Sourdough Hamburger Buns

Makes 6 buns | Bake 375°F for 25 minutes | Rise about 6 hours plus 1 to 2 hours

Back to recipes

Field note

These are soft sourdough buns with enough structure to hold a burger without turning into a sponge. The dough is straightforward, but it needs time to rise properly.

The original recipe builds a little more starter than needed because some sticks to the jar. Practical and mildly irritating, which is most of sourdough.

Ingredients

AmountIngredient
Starter build10g unfed starter + 25g all-purpose flour + 25g water, to yield about 60g active starter
50gactive sourdough starter
360gall-purpose flour
240gwater
30ghoney
10gsalt
1 egg + 1 tbsp wateregg wash
optionalsesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Build the starter. Mix the starter build ingredients in a clean jar. Loosely cover and let rise until doubled and active. You will use 50g in the dough.
  2. Mix the dough. Add the active starter, flour, water, honey, and salt to a large bowl. Mix until there are no dry bits left in the bowl.
  3. Rest 30 minutes. Cover and let the dough rest on the counter for 30 minutes.
  4. Stretch and fold 1. Wet your hand, pick up one side of the dough, stretch it up, and fold it over itself. Turn the bowl and repeat until you have gone around the dough.
  5. Rest 30 minutes. Cover the bowl and let the dough rest.
  6. Stretch and fold 2. Repeat the stretch and fold process.
  7. Rest 30 minutes. Cover and rest again.
  8. Stretch and fold 3. Repeat the final set of stretch and folds. The dough should feel stronger and smoother.
  9. First rise. Cover and let the dough rise until doubled. At about 72°F, plan on roughly 6 hours, but use the dough as the guide.
  10. Optional fridge hold. After the first rise, you can refrigerate the dough for up to 2 days, or continue shaping right away.
  11. Shape. Turn the dough onto a floured surface. Divide into 6 equal pieces, about 112 to 115g each. Shape each piece into a ball and place on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  12. Second rise. Cover and let rise until puffy, about 1 to 2 hours.
  13. Bake. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Brush the buns with egg wash, sprinkle with sesame seeds if using, and bake about 25 minutes until golden.
  14. Cool. Cool before slicing so the crumb can set.

How to tell it is ready

  1. Starter doubled and active before mixing.
  2. First rise dough is doubled.
  3. Second rise buns look puffy and expanded.
  4. Bake tops are golden and buns feel set, not doughy.

Quick recipe version

  1. Build active starter and use 50g.
  2. Mix dough. Rest 30 minutes.
  3. Do 3 stretch and folds, 30 minutes apart.
  4. Rise until doubled, about 6 hours at 72°F.
  5. Divide into 6 buns. Proof 1 to 2 hours until puffy.
  6. Egg wash and bake 375°F for about 25 minutes.

Baking plan checklist

A same-day bun plan based on the recipe: starter build, dough rest, 3 stretch-and-fold sets, first rise, shape, second rise, bake.

Morning, build the starter

  1. Feed starter. Mix 10g unfed starter, 25g flour, and 25g water to make about 60g active starter.
  2. Let rise. Cover loosely and let rise until doubled and active. You will use 50g in the dough.

Late morning or midday, mix and strengthen

  1. Mix dough. Combine 50g active starter, flour, water, honey, and salt until no dry bits remain.
  2. Rest 30 minutes. Cover and leave the dough alone.
  3. Stretch and fold 1. Stretch one side up and fold it over itself, turning the bowl as you go.
  4. Rest 30 minutes.
  5. Stretch and fold 2. Repeat the stretch-and-fold set.
  6. Rest 30 minutes.
  7. Stretch and fold 3. Repeat the final stretch-and-fold set.

Afternoon, rise and shape

  1. First rise. Cover and let rise until doubled. At about 72°F, plan on roughly 6 hours.
  2. Optional fridge hold. After the first rise, refrigerate up to 2 days if you are not baking right away.
  3. Divide. Cut into 6 pieces, about 112 to 115g each.
  4. Shape buns. Shape each piece into a ball and place on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  5. Second rise 1 to 2 hours. Cover and let the buns get puffy.

Bake

  1. Preheat oven. Heat to 375°F.
  2. Egg wash. Brush with egg wash and add sesame seeds if using.
  3. Bake 25 minutes. Bake until golden and set.
  4. Cool before slicing.
Notes: After the first rise, dough can be refrigerated up to 2 days.
