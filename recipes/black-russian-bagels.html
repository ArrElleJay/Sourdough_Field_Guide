Black Russian Bagels

Makes about 12 | Bake 425°F for 20 to 22 minutes | Rise 1 hour plus 20 minutes | Boil 1 minute per side

Black Russian Bagels

Makes about 12 | Bake 425°F for 20 to 22 minutes | Rise 1 hour plus 20 minutes | Boil 1 minute per side

Back to recipes

Field note

These are dark, malty, cocoa-rye bagels with instant yeast. They are not a classic sourdough schedule, and they should not be treated like one.

The dough is darker and a little stickier than a plain bagel dough. Knead until smooth and elastic, then stop. Overworking a low-hydration bagel dough is not a personality test anyone needs.

Ingredients

AmountIngredient
420gbread flour
120gdark rye or pumpernickel flour
340mllukewarm water
100gdark treacle or molasses
30gdark cocoa powder, black cocoa ideal
2 tspinstant yeast
2 tspsalt
optional 1 tbspcaraway seeds
optional 1 tspespresso powder
2 qt / 2Lwater for boiling
2 tbsptreacle or brown sugar for boiling water
optionalpoppy seeds, sesame, onion flakes, or everything seasoning

Instructions

  1. Mix the dry ingredients. In a stand mixer bowl or large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, rye flour, yeast, salt, caraway seeds if using, and espresso powder if using.
  2. Mix the liquid ingredients. In a separate measuring cup, mix the lukewarm water, treacle or molasses, and cocoa powder until combined.
  3. Combine. Pour the liquid mixture into the dry ingredients and mix until a dough forms. Rest for 10 minutes so the flour can hydrate.
  4. Knead. Knead by hand for 6 to 8 minutes, or with a dough hook for 4 to 6 minutes, until smooth and elastic. The dough will be dark and slightly sticky.
  5. First rise. Cover and let rise in a warm spot for about 1 hour, or until doubled.
  6. Divide. Punch down the dough and divide into 12 equal pieces, about 80 to 90g each.
  7. Shape. Shape each piece into a smooth ball. Poke a hole through the center and stretch to about a 1-inch opening.
  8. Short rest. Place bagels on a lined, lightly oiled baking sheet. Cover loosely and rest for 20 minutes.
  9. Prepare water bath. Preheat oven to 425°F. Bring 2 quarts water to a boil and stir in treacle or brown sugar.
  10. Boil. Boil 2 to 3 bagels at a time for 1 minute per side. Remove and let drain briefly.
  11. Top. While wet, add toppings if using.
  12. Bake. Bake for 20 to 22 minutes, until the crust is shiny and dark. Cool on a rack.

How to tell it is ready

  1. Dough smooth, elastic, dark, and slightly sticky.
  2. First rise doubled in size.
  3. After shape bagels have rested and look slightly puffy.
  4. Bake crust is shiny, dark, and set.

Quick recipe version

  1. Mix dry ingredients. Mix water, molasses, and cocoa separately.
  2. Combine and rest 10 minutes. Knead until smooth and elastic.
  3. Rise about 1 hour, until doubled.
  4. Divide into 12, shape bagels, and rest 20 minutes.
  5. Boil 1 minute per side. Add toppings.
  6. Bake 425°F for 20 to 22 minutes.

Baking plan checklist

A same-day yeast bagel plan. This recipe uses instant yeast, not sourdough starter, so the schedule is shorter.

Morning, mix and rise

  1. Mix dry ingredients. Combine bread flour, rye flour, yeast, salt, and optional caraway or espresso.
  2. Mix liquids. Combine lukewarm water, treacle or molasses, and cocoa powder.
  3. Combine dough. Pour liquids into dry ingredients and mix into a dough.
  4. Rest 10 minutes. Let the flour hydrate before kneading.
  5. Knead. Knead until smooth and elastic. The dough will be dark and slightly sticky.
  6. Rise about 1 hour. Cover and keep warm until doubled.

Midday, shape and rest

  1. Divide into 12 pieces. Aim for roughly 80 to 90g each.
  2. Shape bagels. Ball each piece, poke a hole, and stretch into a ring.
  3. Rest 20 minutes. Cover loosely on a lined, lightly oiled baking sheet.

Bake

  1. Preheat oven. Heat to 425°F.
  2. Prepare water bath. Bring 2 quarts water to a boil and stir in treacle or brown sugar.
  3. Boil. Boil 2 to 3 bagels at a time for 1 minute per side.
  4. Top. Add toppings while the bagels are still wet.
  5. Bake 20 to 22 minutes. Bake until shiny, dark, and set.
  6. Cool on a rack.
Notes: Hydration and knead were adjusted after a dry/chewy test batch. Retest before treating as final.
