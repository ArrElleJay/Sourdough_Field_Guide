Sourdough Cheez-It Crackers

Makes 6 servings | Bake 350°F for 15 to 18 minutes | Chill 20 minutes

Sourdough Cheez-It Crackers

Makes 6 servings | Bake 350°F for 15 to 18 minutes | Chill 20 minutes

Back to recipes

Field note

These are discard crackers, which means they should be simple. Blend, chill, roll thin, bake. The important part is the rolling. Thick dough gives you cheesy tiles, not crackers.

The discard does not need to be active. Flat discard is fine here. This is snack math, not bread math.

Ingredients

AmountIngredient
226gcheddar cheese, chopped
85gflat sourdough discard
28gunsalted butter, chopped
5granch seasoning
5gsalt, or half for less salty crackers
65gall-purpose flour
optionalflaky sea salt

Instructions

  1. Make the dough. Blend the cheddar, discard, butter, ranch seasoning, salt, and flour in a food processor until the mixture comes together as a dough.
  2. Chill. Wrap the dough and refrigerate for at least 20 minutes. This firms it up so it is easier to roll thin.
  3. Preheat. Heat the oven to 350°F.
  4. Divide and roll. Split the dough in half. Roll one half very thin on parchment or a silicone baking mat, about 1/8 inch or thinner if you can manage it.
  5. Cut. Cut into roughly 1-inch squares with a pasta cutter, bench scraper, or knife.
  6. Dock. Poke a hole in the middle of each cracker with a toothpick. This helps them bake more evenly and gives the classic cracker look.
  7. Salt. Sprinkle lightly with flaky sea salt if using. Do not separate the crackers.
  8. Bake. Move the parchment or mat to a baking sheet and bake for 15 to 18 minutes. If baking two trays, rotate them halfway through.
  9. Dry and crisp. Turn off the oven and let the crackers sit inside with the door slightly ajar. This helps drive off extra moisture so they crisp instead of staying bendy.
  10. Repeat. Roll, cut, and bake the second half of the dough.

How to tell it is ready

  1. Dough comes together in the food processor and is firm after chilling.
  2. Rolled dough thin enough that it feels almost too thin.
  3. Baked crackers set, lightly browned at the edges, and crisp after drying.
  4. If soft return to the turned-off oven with the door cracked to dry longer.

Quick recipe version

  1. Blend cheese, discard, butter, seasoning, salt, and flour.
  2. Chill at least 20 minutes.
  3. Roll very thin, cut into 1-inch squares, and poke centers.
  4. Bake 350°F for 15 to 18 minutes. Let dry in turned-off oven with door cracked.
Notes: Thin dough is the whole game. When it looks thin enough, make it thinner.
