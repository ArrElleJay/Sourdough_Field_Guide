Sourdough Bagels

Makes 8 | Bake 425°F for 25 to 28 minutes | Ferment 8 to 12 hours plus 30 to 60 minutes

Sourdough Bagels

Makes 8 | Bake 425°F for 25 to 28 minutes | Ferment 8 to 12 hours plus 30 to 60 minutes

Back to recipes

Field note

Bagels are supposed to be stiff, chewy, and a little stubborn. This is not a soft sandwich dough. Do not panic because it feels firm. That is the point.

The overnight room-temperature fermentation gives the dough flavor and makes the morning mostly shaping, boiling, and baking.

Ingredients

AmountIngredient
Starter build15g starter + 50g all-purpose flour + 50g water, to yield enough active starter
100gactive sourdough starter
255gwater
40ghoney or sugar
10gfine sea salt
500gbread flour
6 cupswater for boiling
1 tbspgranulated sugar for boiling water
optionalsesame seeds, everything seasoning, poppy seeds, shredded cheese, or salt

Instructions

  1. Build the starter. About 12 hours before mixing, feed the starter so you have at least 100g active starter. Use it when doubled and active.
  2. Mix the dough. In a stand mixer bowl, stir together the starter, water, honey, and salt. Add the bread flour and bring the dough together as best you can. It will be very stiff.
  3. Knead. Mix with the dough hook on the lowest speed for 6 to 7 minutes, or knead by hand about 10 minutes.
  4. Ferment. Cover the bowl and let the dough rest at room temperature for 8 to 12 hours.
  5. Prepare the pan. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  6. Divide. Turn the dough onto a clean work surface and divide into 8 equal pieces.
  7. Shape. Shape each piece into a ball. Poke a hole through the center with your thumb and gently stretch into a ring. Place on parchment.
  8. Final proof. Cover with a towel and let rise 30 to 60 minutes, until puffy.
  9. Preheat and boil. Preheat the oven to 425°F. Bring 6 cups water to a boil and add 1 tablespoon sugar.
  10. Boil. Boil bagels for 2 minutes per side, working in batches so the pot is not crowded.
  11. Top. Remove with a mesh strainer. When cool enough to handle, dip one side into toppings if using.
  12. Bake. Bake at 425°F for 25 to 28 minutes, until golden brown. Cool on a rack.

How to tell it is ready

  1. Dough stiff but fully mixed and kneaded.
  2. Fermentation dough has rested 8 to 12 hours and looks slightly risen.
  3. Shaped bagels puffy before boiling.
  4. Baked bagels golden and firm with a chewy crust.

Quick recipe version

  1. Build active starter about 12 hours ahead.
  2. Mix stiff dough and knead 6 to 7 minutes by mixer, or 10 minutes by hand.
  3. Ferment 8 to 12 hours.
  4. Divide into 8, shape rings, and proof 30 to 60 minutes.
  5. Boil 2 minutes per side. Add toppings.
  6. Bake 425°F for 25 to 28 minutes.

Baking plan checklist

An overnight sourdough bagel plan: build starter, mix stiff dough, ferment overnight, shape, short proof, boil, bake.

Day 1, morning or afternoon, build the starter

  1. Feed starter. Mix 15g starter, 50g flour, and 50g water to make a little more than the 100g needed.
  2. Let rise about 12 hours. Use when doubled and active.

Day 1, evening, mix and ferment

  1. Mix dough. Stir together active starter, water, honey or sugar, and salt. Add bread flour.
  2. Knead stiff dough. Mix 6 to 7 minutes with a dough hook, or about 10 minutes by hand.
  3. Ferment 8 to 12 hours. Cover and leave at room temperature.

Day 2, morning, shape and proof

  1. Line baking sheet. Use parchment and flour the bottoms if sticking is a problem.
  2. Divide into 8 pieces.
  3. Shape bagels. Shape each into a ball, poke a hole through the center, and stretch into a ring.
  4. Proof 30 to 60 minutes. Cover and let rise until puffy.

Day 2, boil and bake

  1. Preheat oven. Heat to 425°F.
  2. Boil water. Bring 6 cups water to a boil and add 1 tablespoon sugar.
  3. Boil bagels. Boil 2 minutes per side, working in batches.
  4. Add toppings. Dip wet bagels into toppings if using.
  5. Bake 25 to 28 minutes. Bake until golden brown.
  6. Cool on a rack.
Notes: Cinnamon raisin option: add 2 tsp cinnamon to the dry ingredients and knead in 1/2 cup raisins after kneading.
