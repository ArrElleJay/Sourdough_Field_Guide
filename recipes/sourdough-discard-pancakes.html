Sourdough Discard Pancakes

Makes 12 pancakes | Cook medium-low | 2 to 3 minutes first side, 1 to 2 minutes second side

Sourdough Discard Pancakes

Makes 12 pancakes | Cook medium-low | 2 to 3 minutes first side, 1 to 2 minutes second side

Back to recipes

Field note

These are for using discard without pretending breakfast needs a project manager. The batter comes together quickly, and the only real trick is not overmixing it.

Use discard here, not peaked starter. This recipe gets its lift from baking powder and baking soda, so the discard is mostly for flavor and not-wasting-stuff purposes.

Ingredients

AmountIngredient
240gall-purpose flour
8gbaking powder
5gbaking soda
25ggranulated sugar
6gsalt
227gsourdough discard, room temperature
360g2% milk, room temperature
1large egg, beaten, room temperature
28gvegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Whisk the dry ingredients. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt. Break up any obvious lumps so the leaveners are evenly mixed.
  2. Add the wet ingredients. Add the discard, milk, beaten egg, and oil. Mix gently until the batter is combined.
  3. Stop mixing. A few lumps are fine. Smooth pancake batter is usually overmixed pancake batter, and overmixed pancake batter makes sad pancakes.
  4. Heat the pan. Grease a griddle or cast iron skillet with butter or oil and heat over medium-low. Give the pan time to heat evenly before the first pancake.
  5. Cook the pancakes. Use about 1/3 cup batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges begin to look set, about 2 to 3 minutes.
  6. Flip and finish. Flip once and cook the second side for 1 to 2 minutes, until cooked through and lightly browned.
  7. Repeat. Adjust the heat as needed. If the outside is browning before the inside sets, the pan is too hot.

How to tell it is ready

  1. Batter combined but not beaten smooth.
  2. Pan hot enough that batter starts to cook right away, but not so hot it scorches.
  3. First side surface bubbles and edges look set before flipping.
  4. Finished pancake center is cooked through and the pancake springs back lightly.

Quick recipe version

  1. Whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add discard, milk, egg, and oil. Mix gently. Do not beat smooth.
  3. Cook about 1/3 cup batter per pancake over medium-low heat.
  4. Flip when bubbles form and edges set. Cook 1 to 2 minutes more.
Notes: For waffles, use the batter as written or add 1 extra egg if you want a little more structure.
