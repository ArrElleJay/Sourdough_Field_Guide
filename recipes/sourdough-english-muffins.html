Sourdough English Muffins

Makes 10 | Cook low, covered, 4 minutes per side | Ferment 8 to 12 hours plus 1 hour rise

Sourdough English Muffins

Makes 10 | Cook low, covered, 4 minutes per side | Ferment 8 to 12 hours plus 1 hour rise

Back to recipes

Field note

English muffins are cooked on the stove, not baked, so the heat needs to be low and controlled. Too hot and the outside is done while the center is still dough. Rude, but common.

This is an overnight dough. Mix it the night before, let it ferment, then cut and cook in the morning.

Ingredients

AmountIngredient
Starter build15g starter + 50g all-purpose flour + 50g water, to yield enough active starter
100gactive sourdough starter
20ghoney, sugar, or maple syrup
240gmilk
360gall-purpose flour
5gfine sea salt
40gcornmeal, for sprinkling only

Instructions

  1. Build the starter. About 12 hours before mixing, feed the starter so you have at least 100g active starter. The starter should be doubled and active when you use it.
  2. Mix the dough. Add the active starter, sweetener, milk, flour, and salt to a large bowl. Mix until well combined. Do not add the cornmeal to the dough.
  3. Rest. Cover and let the dough rest for 30 to 60 minutes.
  4. Knead. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead by hand for about 5 minutes, or use a stand mixer with dough hook on the lowest speed.
  5. Ferment. Place the dough back in the bowl, cover, and ferment at room temperature for 8 to 12 hours. The original range assumes about 65 to 70°F.
  6. Press and cut. Turn the dough onto a floured surface. Flour the top and press it to about 1/2-inch thickness.
  7. Prepare the pan. Sprinkle a parchment-lined baking sheet with cornmeal. Cut rounds with a 3-inch cutter and place them on the sheet.
  8. Top and rise. Sprinkle the tops with cornmeal, cover with a towel, and let rise for about 1 hour.
  9. Cook. Preheat a nonstick skillet over low heat. Cook the muffins covered, about 4 minutes per side.
  10. Check temperature. The center should register about 200°F when done.

How to tell it is ready

  1. Starter doubled and active before mixing.
  2. Fermented dough expanded and workable after the overnight rest.
  3. Cut muffins puffy after the 1-hour rise.
  4. Cooked muffins about 200°F in the center.

Quick recipe version

  1. Build active starter about 12 hours ahead.
  2. Mix dough, rest 30 to 60 minutes, then knead 5 minutes.
  3. Ferment 8 to 12 hours at room temperature.
  4. Press to 1/2 inch, cut rounds, and coat with cornmeal.
  5. Rise 1 hour. Cook covered on low, about 4 minutes per side, to 200°F inside.
Notes: Warm or humid kitchen adjustment: reduce milk by about 30g and use sugar instead of honey if the dough is too sticky.
