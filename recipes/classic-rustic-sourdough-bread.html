Classic Rustic Sourdough Bread

Makes 1 loaf | 100g active starter | Same-day bake or shaped cold proof

Classic Rustic Sourdough Bread

Makes 1 loaf | 100g active starter | Same-day bake or shaped cold proof

Back to recipes

Field note

This is the basic country loaf: flour, water, salt, and active starter. The recipe is simple, but the timing matters. The dough should look alive before you shape it, and the loaf should cool before you cut it. Annoying, but true.

Use the clock as a plan, not a judge. Temperature, starter strength, and flour choice all change the pace.

Ingredients

AmountIngredient
100gactive sourdough starter
325gwarm water
475gbread flour or high-protein all-purpose flour
10gsalt

Instructions

  1. Feed the starter. Feed far enough ahead that the starter is active when you mix. For many kitchens, that means the night before or early the same morning.
  2. Mix the dough. In a large bowl, mix the active starter and water until loosened. Add the flour and salt. Mix until no dry flour remains.
  3. Rest 30 minutes. Cover the bowl and leave it alone. This gives the flour time to hydrate and makes the dough easier to handle.
  4. Stretch and fold 1. Thirty minutes after mixing, stretch one side of the dough up and fold it over itself. Turn the bowl and repeat until you have gone around the bowl.
  5. Stretch and fold 2. Wait 15 minutes, then repeat.
  6. Stretch and fold 3. Wait another 15 minutes, then repeat.
  7. Stretch and fold 4. Wait another 15 minutes, then repeat. The dough should feel stronger and more organized than when you started.
  8. Bulk ferment. Cover the bowl and let the dough rise until it is noticeably larger, smoother, and puffy. At about 72°F, plan on roughly 5 to 6 hours total from mixing, but watch the dough more than the clock.
  9. Shape. Turn the dough onto the counter. Shape into a tight boule without tearing it. Place seam-side up in a floured proofing basket or towel-lined bowl.
  10. Proof. Cover and proof 1 to 2 hours at room temperature, or refrigerate after shaping if you want to bake later.
  11. Preheat. Heat the oven and Dutch oven to 500°F for about 30 minutes.
  12. Score and bake covered. Place the loaf on parchment, score the top, transfer to the hot Dutch oven, cover, and bake 25 minutes at 500°F.
  13. Finish uncovered. Reduce the oven to 450°F, remove the lid, and bake about 15 minutes more, until the crust is browned and the center is 200 to 210°F.
  14. Cool. Remove the loaf from the Dutch oven and cool fully on a rack before slicing.

How to tell it is ready

  1. Starter: risen predictably and near peak.
  2. Bulk fermentation: dough is puffy, smoother, and larger. It should feel aerated, not dense.
  3. Proof: dough looks relaxed and slightly expanded. If it goes flat or fragile, it waited too long.
  4. Bake: crust is browned and the center reads 200 to 210°F.

Quick recipe version

  1. Use 100g active starter, 325g water, 475g flour, and 10g salt.
  2. Mix everything until no dry flour remains. Rest 30 minutes.
  3. Stretch and fold at 30, 45, 60, and 75 minutes after mixing.
  4. Bulk ferment until puffy and noticeably larger, usually about 5 to 6 hours total at 72°F.
  5. Shape into a boule. Proof 1 to 2 hours, or refrigerate after shaping.
  6. Bake in preheated Dutch oven: 500°F covered for 25 minutes, then 450°F uncovered for about 15 minutes.
  7. Loaf is done at 200 to 210°F inside. Cool fully before slicing.

Baking plan checklist

A generic day-by-day plan for the classic loaf. Use this as the towel-on-the-bowl checklist. The dough still gets the final vote.

Day 1, evening, build the starter

  1. Feed starter. Use the recipe's starter build. For a standard loaf, a 1:3:3 feed works well when you want the starter ready the next morning.
  2. Keep it comfortable. Leave it covered at room temperature, or in a slightly warm spot if your kitchen is cool.

Day 2, morning, mix and strengthen the dough

  1. Check starter. It should be risen and active before you mix.
  2. Mix dough. Combine 100g active starter, 325g water, 475g flour, and 10g salt until no dry flour remains.
  3. Rest 30 minutes. Cover the bowl and leave it alone.
  4. Stretch and fold 1. Do this 30 minutes after mixing.
  5. Wait 15 minutes.
  6. Stretch and fold 2.
  7. Wait 15 minutes.
  8. Stretch and fold 3.
  9. Wait 15 minutes.
  10. Stretch and fold 4.

Day 2, midday to afternoon, bulk and shape

  1. Bulk ferment. Cover and let the dough rise until puffy, smoother, and noticeably larger. Around 72°F, plan on roughly 5 to 6 hours total from mixing.
  2. Shape loaf. Shape into a tight boule without tearing the dough.
  3. Proof. Proof 1 to 2 hours at room temperature, or cover and refrigerate after shaping if baking later.

Bake day

  1. Preheat. Heat oven and Dutch oven to 500°F for about 30 minutes.
  2. Score loaf. Move the loaf to parchment and score the top.
  3. Bake covered. Bake 25 minutes at 500°F.
  4. Bake uncovered. Reduce to 450°F and bake about 15 minutes more, until browned and 200 to 210°F inside.
  5. Cool fully. Put the loaf on a rack and let it cool before slicing.
Notes: Remove the loaf from the Dutch oven immediately after baking so the bottom does not keep cooking against screaming-hot cast iron.
