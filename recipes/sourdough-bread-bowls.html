Sourdough Bread Bowls

Makes 5 to 6 bowls | Bake 450°F for 25 to 30 minutes | Levain overnight plus bulk and cold ferment

Sourdough Bread Bowls

Makes 5 to 6 bowls | Bake 450°F for 25 to 30 minutes | Levain overnight plus bulk and cold ferment

Back to recipes

Field note

Bread bowls need enough crust to hold soup and enough soft center to make the whole thing worth eating. The schedule is longer than a basic loaf because the dough gets a bulk ferment and a cold ferment.

This is one of those recipes where “almost doubled” and “puffy” matter more than the exact minute on the clock. Very annoying. Also true.

Ingredients

AmountIngredient
225gripe active levain, built at 1:10:10
400gwater
600gbread flour
12gsalt
1/2 to 1 cupice cubes for steam

Instructions

  1. Build the levain. Use ripe active levain. The original profile uses a 1:10:10 build, which needs a long lead time, usually overnight.
  2. Mix the dough. Mix the ripe levain, water, bread flour, and salt in a bowl until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Start bulk fermentation. Cover and let the dough sit for 1 hour.
  4. Stretch and fold 1. After the first hour, do a set of stretch and folds by lifting the dough and folding it over itself, turning the bowl as you go.
  5. Rest 1 hour. Cover and let the dough rest.
  6. Stretch and fold 2. Repeat the stretch and fold process.
  7. Rest 1 hour. Cover and rest again.
  8. Stretch and fold 3. Repeat the final set of stretch and folds.
  9. Finish bulk. Cover and let the dough rest about 2 more hours at 78 to 80°F. Look for a 30 to 40% rise, some bubbles, dough pulling from the bowl edges, and a slight dome toward the center.
  10. Cold ferment. Cover and refrigerate overnight. The dough can stay cold for 8 to 48 hours.
  11. Shape. Remove the cold dough from the fridge. Divide into 5 to 6 pieces, about 200 to 240g each. Shape each piece into a tight round.
  12. Final rise. Place rounds on parchment, cover, and let rise 2 to 3 hours, until puffy and almost doubled.
  13. Preheat. Preheat a baking stone at 450°F for 30 to 45 minutes.
  14. Score and steam. Score each dough ball. Place a shallow pan with 1/2 to 1 cup ice on the bottom rack for steam.
  15. Bake. Bake 15 minutes without opening the oven. Remove the steam pan and bake another 10 to 15 minutes, until browned.
  16. Cool and cut. Cool completely before cutting. Cut the tops at an angle and pull out the center, leaving enough crust to hold soup.

How to tell it is ready

  1. Bulk fermentation 30 to 40% rise, scattered bubbles, dough pulling from bowl edges, and a domed center.
  2. Cold dough firm enough to divide and shape.
  3. Final rise rounds are puffy and almost doubled.
  4. Baked bowls browned, firm, and fully cooled before cutting.

Quick recipe version

  1. Use 225g ripe levain, 400g water, 600g bread flour, and 12g salt.
  2. Mix dough. Rest 1 hour.
  3. Do 3 stretch and fold sets, each about 1 hour apart.
  4. Rest about 2 more hours, then refrigerate overnight, 8 to 48 hours.
  5. Divide into 5 to 6 bowls. Rise 2 to 3 hours.
  6. Bake at 450°F with steam for 15 minutes, then 10 to 15 minutes without steam.

Baking plan checklist

A two-day bread bowl plan with levain, bulk fermentation, cold ferment, final rise, steam, and bake.

Day 1, morning, build the levain

  1. Build levain. Mix 10g ripe starter, 110g water, and 110g flour.
  2. Let rise 10 to 12 hours. Keep at warm room temperature, around 78°F, until bubbly, active, and doubled.

Day 1, evening, mix and fold

  1. Mix dough. Combine 225g ripe levain, 400g water, 600g bread flour, and 12g salt.
  2. Rest 1 hour. Cover and let the dough sit.
  3. Stretch and fold 1.
  4. Rest 1 hour.
  5. Stretch and fold 2.
  6. Rest 1 hour.
  7. Stretch and fold 3.
  8. Finish bulk. Let rest about 2 more hours at 78 to 80°F, until 30 to 40% risen with bubbles and a slight dome.
  9. Cold ferment overnight. Cover and refrigerate 8 to 48 hours.

Day 2, shape and rise

  1. Divide cold dough. Cut into 5 to 6 pieces, about 200 to 240g each.
  2. Shape rounds. Shape each piece into a tight round.
  3. Final rise 2 to 3 hours. Place on parchment, cover, and rise until puffy and almost doubled.

Day 2, bake and finish

  1. Preheat stone. Heat baking stone at 450°F for 30 to 45 minutes.
  2. Score bowls. Score each round.
  3. Add steam. Place 1/2 to 1 cup ice in a shallow pan on the bottom rack.
  4. Bake with steam 15 minutes. Do not open the oven.
  5. Remove steam pan. Bake another 10 to 15 minutes until browned.
  6. Cool completely. Cut tops at an angle and remove the center only after cooling.
Notes: No baking stone: use a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake at 450°F for 25 to 30 minutes.
