Pretzel Bites with Sourdough Discard

Makes 32 bites | Bake 500°F for 8 to 10 minutes | Rise 1 to 1 1/2 hours plus 10 to 15 minutes

Pretzel Bites with Sourdough Discard

Makes 32 bites | Bake 500°F for 8 to 10 minutes | Rise 1 to 1 1/2 hours plus 10 to 15 minutes

Back to recipes

Field note

These use discard plus instant yeast, so they are faster and more predictable than a naturally leavened dough. The discard adds flavor, the yeast does the scheduling. Very reasonable of it.

The baking soda wash gives the pretzel bite its pretzel personality without requiring a full boil.

Ingredients

AmountIngredient
145gsourdough discard or bubbly starter
175glukewarm water
5ggranulated sugar
8ginstant yeast
6gsalt
285gall-purpose flour, plus more for rolling
120ghot water for wash
15gbaking soda
55gunsalted butter, melted
optionalflaky salt, parmesan, or cinnamon sugar

Instructions

  1. Mix the dough. In a large bowl or stand mixer bowl, mix the discard, lukewarm water, sugar, yeast, salt, and flour until a dough forms.
  2. Knead. Knead by hand for 5 to 8 minutes, or in a stand mixer for about 5 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth. Add extra flour only 1 tablespoon at a time if the dough is too sticky to handle.
  3. First rise. Lightly oil a bowl, turn the dough to coat, cover, and let rise for 1 to 1 1/2 hours, until doubled.
  4. Prepare the pan. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  5. Divide and cut. Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and divide it into 4 pieces. Roll each piece into a long strand and cut each strand into 6 to 8 bites.
  6. Make the baking soda wash. Stir the baking soda into the hot water until completely dissolved. It may bubble a little. That is fine.
  7. Coat the bites. Brush each pretzel bite with the baking soda wash, or quickly dunk each piece and place it back on the parchment.
  8. Preheat and short rise. Preheat the oven to 500°F. Let the pretzel bites rise for 10 to 15 minutes while the oven heats.
  9. Bake. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes, until the outsides are browned and crisp.
  10. Butter and finish. Brush or toss the hot bites with all of the melted butter. Add salt, parmesan, or cinnamon sugar while the butter is still wet.

How to tell it is ready

  1. Dough smooth, slightly tacky, and able to form a ball without leaving heavy sticky residue.
  2. First rise doubled in size.
  3. After shaping bites look slightly puffy before baking.
  4. Baked bites browned outside and cooked through inside.

Quick recipe version

  1. Mix discard, water, sugar, yeast, salt, and flour.
  2. Knead until smooth. Rise 1 to 1 1/2 hours, until doubled.
  3. Divide into 4 strands and cut 32 bites.
  4. Brush or dunk with baking soda wash. Rest 10 to 15 minutes.
  5. Bake 500°F for 8 to 10 minutes. Butter and top immediately.

Baking plan checklist

A same-day discard pretzel plan using instant yeast for the rise, then baking soda wash and a hot bake.

Mix and rise

  1. Mix dough. Combine sourdough discard, lukewarm water, sugar, instant yeast, salt, and flour.
  2. Knead 5 to 8 minutes. Knead until the dough comes together in a smooth ball. Add flour only as needed.
  3. Rise 1 to 1 1/2 hours. Lightly oil the bowl, cover, and let rise.

Shape and wash

  1. Prepare baking sheet. Line with parchment.
  2. Divide dough. Punch down, divide into 4 sections, roll each into a strand, and cut into 8 pieces.
  3. Make baking soda wash. Heat 120g water until almost boiling and dissolve 15g baking soda.
  4. Brush or dunk. Coat each pretzel bite with the baking soda wash.
  5. Preheat oven. Heat to 500°F.
  6. Rest 10 to 15 minutes. Let the bites rise while the oven heats.

Bake and finish

  1. Bake 8 to 10 minutes. Bake until crisp and lightly browned.
  2. Melt butter. Prepare butter and toppings while the bites bake.
  3. Brush with butter. Use all the butter. This is not the moment for restraint.
  4. Add toppings. Finish with salt, parmesan, or cinnamon sugar.
  5. Eat warm.
Notes: It will feel like too much butter. It is not too much butter.
